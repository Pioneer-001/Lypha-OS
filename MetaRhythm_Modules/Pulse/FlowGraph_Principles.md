## 1. Definition — What is FlowGraph?

**FlowGraph** is the structural map that explains:

- how a **flow (Flow)**
- becomes **structure (Z, Structure)**
- and is finally compressed into a **coordinate (TP, Coordinate)**

in a single coherent diagram and principle set.

Another way to say it:

> "The engine that describes *everything before* the coordinate is formed —
> as structure."

If TP is the **completed coordinate**, FlowGraph is the **layer that handles all pre-coordinate flow**.

---

## 2. Principle 1 — Flow as a Vector

Flow is not just "emotional movement."  
It is defined as the vector sum of **Structure (Z), Emotion (E), and Time (Y)**.

```text
Flow = Z(direction) + E(intensity) + Y(sequence)
```

- **Z (direction)**: macro direction, structural trend, high-level axis
- **E (intensity)**: emotion, energy, level of focus, psychological charge
- **Y (sequence)**: temporal order, rhythm, unfolding pattern

FlowGraph always looks at these three axes together.  
Not "only emotion", not "only structure", not "only time" —
**but the combination of all three.**

Examples:
- The full regime shift of a market (e.g., low-rate → high-rate → AI phase)
- The life cycle of a project (idea → build → launch → scale)

---

## 3. Principle 2 — Entry Order (Z → Y → E → X → V → Z’)

FlowGraph is the real-time application of the Lypha-OS base philosophy **Z–Y–X + V**.

```text
Z   →   Y    →    E    →    X    →    V    →    Z’
Structure   Time      Emotion      Reality    Verified   Updated Structure
```

- **Z**: pre-defined structure, direction, system
- **Y**: how that structure is revealed through time
- **E**: emotional and energetic waves emerging in the process
- **X**: visible events, prices, actions, behavior at the surface
- **V**: the point where results are recorded and verified
- **Z’**: the updated structure after V has been reflected

FlowGraph acts as the **philosophical layer that enforces this order** so it does not collapse.

## 4. Principle 3 — Collapse into One Word (Essence Word)

FlowGraph has a very simple completion criterion:

> **When a massive flow can be compressed into one word,  
> the structuralization of that flow is complete.**

Examples:

- Multi‑year inflation → high‑rate cycle → policy shock → → `rates`
- A decade of an industry’s evolution (e.g., cloud, EV, AI) → `adoption` or `transition`
- A person’s career path (student → worker → founder) → `growth` or `alignment`

FlowGraph treats the act of finding this Essence Word as **"axis extraction."**

```text
Flow (large wave) ──▶ Essence Word (axis)
```

This single word then becomes the **coordinate keyword** that guides TP, strategy, narratives, threads, and execution.

## 5. Principle 4 — Emotion → Pulse → Structure Bridge

FlowGraph is the bridge between **Emotion (E)** and **Structure (Z)**.  
It connects directly to the Emotion Router / Emotion Circuit.

The conversion pipeline is always defined as:

```text
Emotion (E) → Pulse (Language) → Structure (Z)
```

1. **Emotion (E)**  
   - Pre-verbal feelings: tension, discomfort, excitement, fixation, pain

2. **Pulse (Language)**  
   - Compressed linguistic waves: tweets, one-line notes, short messages, key phrases

3. **Structure (Z)**  
   - Stabilized forms: manifestos, OS design, strategy docs, TP frameworks, season definitions

FlowGraph_Principles.md **locks this bridge at the principle level** for Lypha OS.

## 6. Principle 5 — Output: Path (Direction, Timing, Coordinate)

The final output FlowGraph must generate is a single object:

```text
Path = (Direction, Timing, Coordinate)
```

- **Direction**: Where should we go? (e.g., long/short, approach/exit, deepen/exit)
- **Timing**: When should we move? (now / wait / abandon, or by season / event / candle)
- **Coordinate (TP)**: At exactly which spot and under what conditions do we enter/express?

Within TP 2.0’s **Direction–Space–Time** structure,  
FlowGraph is responsible for designing the path **from Direction + Time → Coordinate**.
